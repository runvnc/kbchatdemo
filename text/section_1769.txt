Sec. 161.132. CIVIL SUIT AGAINST NONRESIDENT VIOLATOR. (a) If
a person who commits an offense under Section 161.135, 161.136,
161.137, 161.138, 161.141, or 161.143 of this code is not a resident
of this state, is a foreign corporation not permitted to do business
in this state, or is absent from this state at the time the offense
is committed, the county attorney of the county in which the
violation occurs shall sue that person for collection of the fine
provided for the offense. In addition, the county attorney shall
seek to attach that person's property in this state and, after final
judgment, have the attached property sold under execution for the
purpose of paying the fine and costs of suit.
(b) A suit under this section shall be brought in the name of
the State of Texas and the court may not require a cost or attachment
bond.
Acts 1981, 67th Leg., p. 1404, ch. 388, Sec. 1, eff. Sept. 1, 1981.
