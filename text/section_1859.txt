Sec. 167.001. DEFINITIONS. In this chapter:
(1) "Animal" means any domestic, free-range, or wild animal
capable of hosting or transporting ticks capable of carrying Babesia,
including:
(A) livestock;
(B) zebras, bison, and giraffes; and
(C) deer, elk, and other cervid species.
(1-a) "Commission" means the Texas Animal Health
Commission.
(2) "Enclosure" includes a pasture, pen, or lot.
(3) "Inspector" means an inspector of the commission,
including a local inspector, a county or district supervising
inspector, and the chief inspector.
(4) "Livestock" means cattle, horses, mules, jacks, or
jennets.
(5) "Peace officer" includes a sheriff, constable, or other

(6) "Tick" means any tick capable of carrying Babesia,

(8) "Treatment" means a procedure or management practice
used on an animal to prevent the infestation of, control, or
eradicate ticks capable of carrying Babesia.
