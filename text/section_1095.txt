Sec. 80.020. AUTHORITY TO PROHIBIT PLANTING OF CITRUS AND
REQUIRE PARTICIPATION IN SUPPRESSION PROGRAM. (a) The commissioner
may adopt reasonable rules regarding areas where citrus may not be
planted in a pest management zone if there is reason to believe
planting will jeopardize the success of the program or present a
hazard to public health or safety.
(b) The commissioner may adopt rules requiring all growers of
citrus in a pest management zone to participate in a pest suppression
program and growers of commercial citrus to participate in pest and
disease management programs that include cost sharing as required by
the rules.
(c) Notice of a prohibition or requirement shall be given by
publication for one day each week for three successive weeks in a
newspaper having general circulation in the affected area.
(d) The commissioner may adopt a reasonable schedule of penalty
fees to be assessed against growers in a designated pest management
zone who do not meet the requirements of the rules issued by the
commissioner relating to reporting of acreage and participation in
cost sharing. A penalty fee may not exceed $50 per acre.
Added by Acts 2009, 81st Leg., R.S., Ch. 506 (S.B. 1016), Sec. 10.01,
eff. September 1, 2009.
Amended by:
Acts 2015, 84th Leg., R.S., Ch. 29 (S.B. 1749), Sec. 10, eff.
