Sec. 161.111. DEFINITION. In this subchapter, "livestock
market" means a stockyard, sales pavilion, or sales ring where
livestock, exotic livestock, or exotic fowl are assembled or
concentrated at regular or irregular intervals for sale, trade,
barter, or exchange.
Acts 1981, 67th Leg., p. 1402, ch. 388, Sec. 1, eff. Sept. 1, 1981.
Amended by Acts 1995, 74th Leg., ch. 554, Sec. 22, eff. Sept. 1,
1995.
