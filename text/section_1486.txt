Sec. 132.084. MISLEADING ADVERTISING. (a) A person commits an
offense if the person:
(1) advertises or sells shell eggs below the quality of
Grade A by describing the eggs as "fresh," "yard," "selected,"
"hennery," "new-laid," "infertile," "cage," or with words that have
similar meaning; or
(2) advertises eggs by price without also indicating the
full, correct, and unabbreviated designation of size and grade of the
eggs.
(b) An offense under this section is a misdemeanor punishable
by a fine of not less than $50 nor more than $1,000.
