Sec. 301.253. GROUNDS FOR REVOCATION OR SUSPENSION OF LICENSE.
(a) This section applies to a license issued under this chapter or
under a rule adopted under this chapter.
(b) After notice and hearing, the Texas Department of Licensing
and Regulation may suspend or revoke a license, place on probation a
person whose license has been suspended, reprimand a license holder,
or refuse to renew or reissue a license on any of the following
grounds:
(1) having a record of environmental violations in the
preceding five years at a permit site;
(2) committing fraud or deceit in obtaining the license;
(3) demonstrating gross negligence, incompetency, or

(4) making an intentional misstatement or misrepresentation

(6) being indebted to the state for a fee, payment of a

Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation; or
(7) failing to continue to possess qualifications necessary
for the issuance of the license.
